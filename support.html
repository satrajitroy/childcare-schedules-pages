Local scheduling and device limits

ChildCare Schedules runs locally on your device. The app does not send schedule data to a server.

Most normal childcare schedules are phone-scale. Very large schedules, especially schedules with more than 100 staff/workers and covered breaks, lunch, prep, or other off-floor obligations, can require a large amount of memory. On iPhone, iOS may stop a heavy local run because of memory pressure or thermal protection. This is a device resource limit, not a scheduling-rule or soundness failure.

To avoid device termination, follow the in-app phone-scale warnings. For very large covered-duty schedules, try reducing covered off-floor obligations, using built-in cover where appropriate, or testing a smaller scenario first.

Verification note

ChildCare Schedules uses a formally verified scheduling core. For bounded runs, any returned schedule is sound with respect to the encoded rules for that run.

The app does not claim completeness for every bounded run. Because practical search limits are used, “no schedule found” may mean no schedule was found within the configured bounds, not necessarily that no schedule exists mathematically.

Data export

Schedules and profiles stay on your device unless you choose to export or share them using iOS sharing features.
